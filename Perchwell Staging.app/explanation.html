PerchPrice: The Number that Matters

PerchPrice Overview

PerchPrice is a proprietary metric that encapsulates, standardizes, and contextualizes the key financial characteristics and considerations of a home. As buyers know or will quickly learn, two major factors define the financial picture of a residential property:

  1. Asking Price.
  2. Monthlies (ongoing costs of ownership including maintenance/common charges and property taxes).

Understanding and evaluating how these two factors a) interact with one another and b) define the landscape of what homes truly cost is essential to finding the home that is the perfect fit for your unique preferences and circumstances. We developed PerchPrice to facilitate that process. We get into a lot more detail below, but in short:

PerchPrice = Asking Price + Present Value of Monthlies

Asking Price is straightforward – it is the listing’s sticker price. Present Value of Monthlies is the estimated value today of the future ongoing costs of owning the home. It is derived primarily from listing information such as maintenance/common charges and property taxes, as well as from public financial and tax records.

PerchPrice Brings Order to a lot of information

Asking Price and Present Value of Monthlies are not independent of one another; they are in fact quite related. Like any feature or characteristic of a home, the price a buyer would be willing to pay for that home today is dependent on the ongoing costs of owning that home. All else equal, the lower the ongoing costs of owning a particular home, the more a buyer would be willing to pay for that home today.

In New York, the ongoing costs of home ownership are made up of common/maintenance charges and property taxes. These costs vary substantially building to building based on factors including amenities, location, physical condition, age, and financial position. For example:

  1. A low-rise walkup without any amenities has significantly different operating costs (paid by the owners of the building through maintenance/common charges) than a luxury high-rise.
  2. Similarly, a building in poor physical condition that requires frequent and costly repairs will likely have higher maintenance/common charges than an otherwise similar building in perfect shape.

In light of these concepts, PerchPrice synthesizes real estate data with a powerful platform that enables potential buyers to easily understand what they are paying for, how they are paying for it, and what tradeoffs they are making in the process.

The Power Behind Perchwell’s Platform

PerchPrice is the product of consolidating, synthesizing, and rendering searchable millions of data points from various sources. Though related and highly relevant to the process of searching for a home, these data have not been accessible in such a format until now. Before Perchwell, accessing and making sense of these data was a painstaking and manual process for even a single home. Searching all listings in the context of these data was not possible.

To enable this search experience and, specifically, calculate PerchPrice we take into account data including:

  1. Listing information (including asking price, maintenance/common charges).
  2. New York City property tax information (including tax rates, taxable values of properties, commercial exemptions/abatements).
  3. User-specific assumptions (including income tax implications and time value of money).

What PerchPrice is NOT

  1. An attempt to value/appraise any property.
    1. We are not making any assumptions or predictions about what any property could/should sell for at any point.
    2. On the contrary, we are simply providing a standardized encapsulation of the multi-faceted pricing of homes.
  2. An investment recommendation.
    1. Similar to the above, PerchPrice does not comment on a property’s investment merits.
    2. Instead, PerchPrice concisely make sense of key data to allow you to more effectively interact with those data.
    3. That said, our users can and do use Perchwell to form/evaluate investment theses… we simply aim to give our users the tools and information required to accomplish whatever real estate goals they have.
  3. Any representation of mortgage financing.
    1. Mortgage availability is determined on a case by case basis and is independent of the search product we have built.
    2. Please use the mortgage calculator on our listing detail pages for help in this area.

For Those Who Are Really Curious…

…or who just cannot figure out why we are so into this:

This seems like it is a lot of work, right? It is; and we are very proud of what we are building because we know it will really help you out. A few stats to help illustrate why:

  1. 15% to 40% of a typical New York apartment’s PerchPrice is a product of factors other than Asking Price.
  2. A $100 variance in Monthlies between two otherwise identical apartments equates to $15,000 to $20,000 in value…
  3. …and Monthlies vary a lot – currently an average Manhattan 1BR listing has ~$1,500 in Monthlies, while the standard deviation of Monthlies for those listings is ~$1,600.

How Perchwell calculates Present Value of Monthlies:

We believe that you should not need to be a real estate/financial/tax/legal professional in order to access and understand this stuff. So we are trying to make the process as seamless and interactive as possible.

Inputs that go into the calculation:

  1. Overall building characteristics:
    1. Commercial Tax Benefit Status – if a building has a 421A, 421G, or J51 commercial tax benefit in place, the property tax profiles of its units are impacted (often significantly) for the duration of the benefit.
    2. Ownership Structure – whether a building is a condo, co-op, or townhouse impacts residents’ ongoing costs of owning their home.
  2. Individual unit characteristics:
    1. Common Charges – applicable to units in condo buildings.
    2. Maintenance Fees (and the tax deductibility of those fees) – applicable to units in co-op buildings.
    3. Property Taxes – applicable to units in condo buildings and to townhouses.
  3. User-specific assumptions (Perchwell defaults to a set of assumptions for the “average” buyer, which you can manipulate to further personalize and refine your results if desired):
    1. Discount Rate – the basic “time value of money” concept is that a dollar today is worth more than a dollar tomorrow. How much more (i.e. the discount rate) depends on a lot of things, including your personal situation (Perchwell defaults to 8%).
    2. Income Tax Deduction Capture – the percentage of the potential income tax deductions associated with the property that you think you will be able to capture (Perchwell defaults to 100%).
    3. Marginal Tax Rate – your marginal income tax rate (Perchwell defaults to 40%).

The calculation itself:

If you have read this far, thank you. We were not sure anyone would. And if you have made it this far, you probably will not be surprised to learn that the exact equation here varies depending on a few things (namely the overall building characteristics) and can get pretty involved. In its simplest form:

Present Value of Monthlies = After Tax Cost of Monthlies / (Discount Rate – Inflation)

After Tax Cost of Monthlies is a function of Income Tax Deduction Capture, Marginal Tax Rate, and (if applicable) Commercial Tax Benefit Status, Ownership Structure, Common Charges, Maintenance Fees, Property Taxes.

Inflation is assumed to be 2.5%.
